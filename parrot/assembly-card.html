  • Speaker 28 mm · 234920 · 1×
  • JST-PH 2.0 pigtail · 1181279 · 3×
  • Magnets 10×1.5 mm · 1186978 · pack of 10

Botland (order #1618961, ~43 PLN)

  • F-F jumper ribbon · 19620 · 1× (use 6)
  • USB-A→USB-C cable · 14829 · 2×

Printstein (~65 PLN)

  • body_top.stl (matte light grey PLA)
  • belly_plate.stl
  • feet.stl (×2)

User-sourced

  • USB power bank (Wozinsky WPower 10000 or existing)
  • Brooch bar pin ~38 mm (pasmanteria)

② Tools

Required: CA (super) glue · scissors or wire strippers · PVC insulation tape.
Optional: multimeter · Posca paint markers (white/black/red).
Not needed: soldering iron · 3D printer · crimper.

③ Assembly steps

  1. Flash XIAO first (before gluing) — see back panel.
  2. Glue magnets: 2 in belly plate (N up/out), 2 in body_top floor (N down/in). CA glue, hold 20 s. Dry-fit: belly plate should snap shut.
  3. Glue brooch bar pin into rear recess on belly plate (external). Hold 30 s, cure 10 min.
  4. Friction-fit feet into belly plate holes (dab CA glue if loose).
  5. Speaker→JST pigtail: strip 5 mm, twist pairs, wrap with tape. Tug-test.
  6. Wire XIAO ↔ MAX98357A (6 F-F jumpers, see wiring table).
  7. Plug JST plug into MAX98357A PH2.0 socket.
  8. Mount boards in body_top cavity with 2 cm² foam tape (camera bore at front, USB-C at tail).
  9. Drop speaker into hex grille pocket (belly-facing).
  10. Close belly plate — magnets snap shut.
  11. Thread USB-C cable through tail slot into XIAO port.
  12. Plug into power bank — double-press flashlight button to lock always-on.
  13. Pin through shirt shoulder seam via brooch bar.

④ Wiring: XIAO ↔ MAX98357A

XIAOGPIOMAX98357AWhat
3V3—VIN+3.3 V supply

GND—SDtie LOW (enables amp)
D12BCLKI²S bit clock

Gotcha: leaving SD floating silently mutes the amp — the #1 "no audio" cause. GAIN pin stays unconnected (default 9 dB is fine).

Part 2 — Flash · Boot · First Chat

⑤ Flashing the XIAO

Do this BEFORE gluing. Confirm the board is alive first.
  1. Install PlatformIO: brew install platformio
  2. Find Mac IP: ipconfig getifaddr en0 (wired) or en1 (WiFi)
  3. Copy template + edit: 
    cd /Users/kolinko/claude/varia/parrot/firmware
cp src/secrets.h.template src/secrets.h
# edit src/secrets.h:
#   WIFI_SSID, WIFI_PASS
#   MAC_BRAIN_HOST = (IP from step 2)
#   MAC_BRAIN_PORT = 8766
  4. Plug XIAO via USB-C into Mac. First flash: hold BOOT, tap RESET, release BOOT.
  5. Compile + upload: 
    pio run
pio run -t upload
pio device monitor -b 115200
  6. Look for: ws: connected — that's the green light.

⑥ Start the Mac brain

Run preflight first — catches all Mac-side problems before you flash: 
python3 parrot/brain/preflight.py
Want all PASS. Then: 
cd /Users/kolinko/claude/varia/parrot/brain
./run.sh            # or: ./run.sh dry (no TTS)

⑦ First chat

  1. Pin parrot to shoulder · plug power bank · lock always-on.
  2. Say: "Hey parrot, can you see me?"
  3. Within ~6–8 s, expect a reply through the speaker.
  4. Watch admin panel: kex7m.effort.ws/parrot
Expected behaviour:
· Reactive = replies when you speak
· Proactive = checks every 5 min, talks ≤ once per 20 min
· May say nothing intentionally — it's a companion, not a chatbot

⑨ Safety

  • Indoor only — not waterproof.
  • Magnets are strong — away from cards / pacemakers.
  • Camera privacy: unplug in confidential rooms (no mic mute in v1).
  • LAN-only ws:// — don't take on hotel WiFi.
